# AI Integrity — Claims & Citations Policy

Same standard as The Digital Law Firm (Law Society Publishing).

## Zero Fabrication Rule

Every factual claim on the website must be:
1. **Sourced** — name the source and year inline or in a footnote
2. **Verified** — checked against the primary source, not paraphrased from a secondary
3. **Current** — dated; regulatory positions change quarterly

If a claim cannot be sourced, it is either removed or marked `[UNVERIFIED — pending source]`.

## Regulatory Text

SRA, FCA, ICO, ICAEW, ACCA, ARB, RIBA, RICS regulatory text must be:
- Quoted verbatim from the primary source (sra.org.uk, fca.org.uk, ico.org.uk, etc.)
- Cited with URL and access date
- Never paraphrased — paraphrasing regulatory text changes its meaning

## Statistics

- State the source report and year: "SRA Risk Outlook, 2025"
- Use `~` prefix for approximations: "~14% of sole practitioners" not "14%"
- Do not cite statistics from vendor press releases as independent data

## Sector Examples / Case Studies

All case studies on the website are either:
- Real, with explicit member permission (name or anonymised as agreed)
- Marked `[COMMUNITY CASE STUDY — to be added from member submissions after launch]`

Fabricated or illustrative examples are not permitted without a visible disclaimer.

## Competitor References

Competitor pricing and positioning claims (e.g. The AI Consultancy £1,500–£8,000) must:
- Link to the primary source URL
- Include the verification date
- Be removed if the source changes

## What This Covers

This policy applies to:
- All website copy (hero, sector pages, pricing, book credibility section)
- Community content (templates, tool reviews, implementation guides)
- Social media posts referencing statistics or regulatory positions
- Any published material under the AI Integrity brand

## Review Cycle

Regulatory references reviewed quarterly (March, June, September, December).
Any claim flagged as potentially outdated is marked for review before the next cycle.
